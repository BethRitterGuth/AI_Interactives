Day 3: Silence

The Gift of Light

Today's Saint

St. Brigid, a beacon in the Celtic tradition, is celebrated as a bringer of light and wisdom. Her story intertwines with the ancient rhythms of the earth, bridging the old ways with the new faith. She embodies the warmth of the hearth and the first light of spring, a symbol of hope in the darkest of winter.

Celtic Prayer

"Circle me, Lord,
Keep protection near
And danger afar.

Circle me, Lord,
Keep hope within.
Keep doubt without.

Circle me, Lord,
Keep light near
And darkness afar."

Deep peace of the quiet earth to you,
Deep peace of the still air to you,
Deep peace of the silence of night to you,
Deep peace of the heart’s quiet to you.
May the God of stillness be your companion this day.

Be a bright flame before me, O God,
Be a guiding star above me,
Be a smooth path below me,
And be a quiet stillness within me,
Today, tonight, and forever.

Leave me alone with You, O God,
Let the noise of the world fall away,
Let my heart be still,
Let my soul be quiet,
That I may hear the whisper of Your love.

In the turning of the year,
In the hush between heartbeats,
In the space where words end and listening begins,
May the Holy One meet you in the silence.

O God who makes the wild goose fly in silence,
Who stills the storm with a word unspoken,
Quiet my heart, calm my mind,
And let me rest in the great silence that is You.

Christ in the silence of my heart,
Christ in the stillness of this moment,
Christ in the quiet of my breathing,
Christ in me, Christ around me,
Christ in the hush, now and evermore.

As the deer seeks the quiet waters,
So my soul longs for Your stillness, O Lord.
In the fading light and the gathering dark,
Grant me the gift of sacred silence.
